BIG DATA ANALYSIS

Definition

Big data often includes data sets of a size that far outstrip the capabilities of conventional software tools to collect, display, manage and process data for an acceptable period of time. Large data size is a constantly changing goal. As of 2012, the range is a few dozen terabytes to many petabytes of data. Large data requires a new set of techniques and technologies to be explored from diverse, complex, and large data sets


Wikipedia user activity is modeled and with terabytes in size, this can also be considered a Big Data form.

In 2009, Google used its Big Data data to analyze and predict the impact of the H1N1 flu pandemic. This service is called Google Flu Trends. Featured

Big Data is characterized by the following characteristics:

Volume

The amount of data created and stored. The size of the data determines the value and potential insight- and whether it can actually be considered as large data.

Variety

Types and types of data. Data is collected from a variety of sources and data types also have a variety of structures.

Velocity

In this case, the speed of the data is created and processed to meet the needs and challenges of growth and development.

Veracity (Authenticity)

The quality of the data obtained can vary greatly, affecting the accuracy of the analysis.
