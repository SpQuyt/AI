Top 10 Digital Transformation Trends in 2018

1. IoT

The IoT will push us to the edge.

2. 5G

Just as the amount of data produced by the IoT will force data to the edge, it will also force mobile providers to move faster than ever — toward 5G.

3. Analytics

The mass amount of information being created by the IoT has the power to revolutionize everything from manufacturing and healthcare to the layout and functioning of entire cities.

4. Edge Computing

Computing technology is pushed to "edge computing," the edge of the network, the perimeter where the Internet ends and the real world begins. Data centers are in the "center" of the network, while personal computers, phones and surveillance cameras are at the edge.

5. Blockchain

Blockchain is a technology that allows secure data transmission based on extremely complex coding systems. While its “famous brother” continues to blow away stock market analysts, Blockchain finally find its place in 2018. From the February of 2017, Blockchain was the second top search term on its website, increasing 400% in just 12 months .

6. AI

With everyone from toddlers to seniors using Alexa, Siri, and customer service chatbots, it’s no wonder AI may soon begin to feel like old news—at least to mainstream users. On the business side, however, so much power remains in AI — in everything from customer service and robotics to analytics and marketing.

7. Virtual Reality: from hero to zero

Just yesterday, tons of us were getting excited about buying our first set of VR headsets – and out of nowhere. AR has pushed it out of the way. Why ? At least for now, it’s simply cheaper and easier to use. By using 3D visualization, companies can better tranin, pitch and envision new product – without the same expense of VR.

8. Failure as a Service?

Just as we saw the as-a-Service market continue to grow with software, infrastructure, and – just about everything else – failure will soon be a service on the aaS menu. The reason: failinh fast iss one of the most important elements of success in today’s quick-moving digital transformation environment.

9. Culture remains a hurdle

If your company is battling culture issues in digital transformation, they will struggle even harder in the future. As the pace of change continues to increase – and the number of new technologies continue to grow – it will become even more imperative that companies move fast while moving forward toward growth.

10. Digital Transformation “becomes a must”

From 2018, the reality will force many companies to realized Digital Transformation is not joke. It’s an imperative in today’s bussiness market. Disruption will continue to be an increasingly common occurrence in the next few years, and companies unable or unprepared for those changes will quickly fall to the bottom of the pack.
